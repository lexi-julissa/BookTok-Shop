"They Both Die at the End" is a gripping and emotionally charged young adult novel written by Adam Silvera. Set in a near-future world where individuals receive a phone call alerting them of their impending death, the story follows two teenage boys, Mateo and Rufus, who receive this ominous call on the same day. Facing the certainty of their demise within the next twenty-four hours, Mateo and Rufus connect through an app called Last Friend, designed to help people find companionship on their final day.

As Mateo and Rufus navigate their last day alive, they embark on a journey filled with introspection, adventure, and unexpected moments of joy. Despite their initial differences, the two boys form a profound bond as they confront their fears, regrets, and dreams. Through their shared experiences, they discover the true meaning of friendship, love, and living life to the fullest, even in the face of mortality.

Silvera's poignant narrative delves into themes of acceptance, resilience, and the fleeting nature of life. With its heartfelt exploration of human connection and the inevitability of death, "They Both Die at the End" is a compelling and thought-provoking tale that resonates long after the final page is turned.
